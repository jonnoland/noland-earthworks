## Ad Types That Work for Land Clearing (Industry Research)

### Top Performing Ad Formats

1. **Before/After Photo Ads** — Single image showing overgrown property vs. cleared result. Most effective creative type. Stops the scroll because property owners see their own problem reflected.

2. **Problem/Solution Hook Ads** — Opens with the problem the landowner has (overgrown fence line, can't use their land, fire hazard), then presents forestry mulching as the clean solution. No burn piles, no hauling, no erosion.

3. **Education Ads** — Explains what forestry mulching actually is and why it's better than bush hogging or bulldozing. Targets people who don't know the service exists yet.

4. **Seasonal Urgency Ads** — "Fall/winter is the best time to clear — dormant vegetation, firmer ground, faster results. Book now before the calendar fills up."

5. **Veteran-Owned Trust Ads** — Leads with veteran-owned identity, reliability, and "we show up when we say we will." Resonates strongly in rural Tennessee.

6. **Reclaim Your Land Ads** — Emotional angle: "You bought this land for a reason. Let's make it usable again." Targets landowners who have been putting off clearing.

7. **Specific Use Case Ads** — Targets a specific job type: pasture reclamation for farmers, fence line clearing, lot clearing for developers, right-of-way clearing.

## What Doesn't Work (Avoid)
- Generic "Call us for land clearing" with no hook
- Stock images of forests or equipment in parking lots
- Broad targeting (renters, urban areas, small suburban lots)
- Corporate language ("solutions," "dedicated team," "passionate about")
- Overly long copy without a clear hook in the first line
