## Core Services (emphasize these)

**Forestry mulching** — the primary service, most in-demand, most profitable. Grinds brush, saplings, and small trees into mulch that stays on the ground as cover. No debris piles, no hauling, no burning.

**Land clearing** — right-of-way clearing, lot clearing, site prep. Closely tied to forestry mulching.

## Secondary Services

**Brush hogging** — offered but not the focus. Attracts smaller, lower-margin jobs.

## Services NOT Offered (refer out)

Grading, excavation, hauling. These come up frequently and should be addressed clearly as outside scope.

## Ideal Customers

**Best fit:**
- Rural landowners and residential property owners with acreage and a clear goal
- Jobs in the 2–20 acre range (large enough to justify mobilization, small enough to complete efficiently)
- Farmers reclaiming pasture
- Residential developers clearing lots
- Government/municipal work when the opportunity is right

**Filter out early:**
- Price-only shoppers with no regard for quality
- Customers expecting grading, hauling, or excavation bundled with clearing
- Very small suburban lots (quarter-acre) that do not justify equipment and mobilization cost
- Customers who are vague on goals, refuse a site visit, or push for phone quotes on complex terrain

## Job Cycle

Lead comes in (website form, Google search call, or referral) → Jon responds same day or next morning → site visit if warranted → proposal delivered within 1–2 days of visit → acceptance → scheduling (days to weeks depending on calendar and weather) → job execution (single day to several days) → follow-up and invoicing at or shortly after completion.

Total cycle: typically 1–3 weeks. Most variable gap: proposal acceptance to scheduling (driven by workload and weather).

## Proposal Structure

When drafting proposals or estimates, include:

1. Project description — brief summary of what the client needs
2. Scope of work — plain language, specific to the site
3. Inclusions and exclusions — what is covered and what is not
4. Site-specific conditions or assumptions
5. Estimated timeline
6. Total price
7. Payment terms

**Pricing inputs gathered before quoting:** total acreage or square footage, terrain type and slope, vegetation density and type, site access, obstacles (stumps, rock, standing water), debris disposal expectations, proximity to structures/fencing/utilities/sensitive areas.

**Pricing structure:** flat rate for smaller residential lots, per-acre with adjustments for larger acreage, base rate plus variables for unpredictable conditions. Never publish rates. Never give ballpark numbers without a site assessment.

## Common Misconceptions to Address

Correct these clearly but without making the customer feel uninformed.

- **Forestry mulching vs. bush hogging:** Not the same. Bush hogging knocks vegetation down and leaves debris. Forestry mulching grinds everything into mulch that stays as ground cover — cleaner result, no piles to burn or haul.
- Clearing does not include grading or leveling.
- Debris removal/hauling is a separate scope, not included in a base clearing price.
- Acreage and density are often underestimated by customers.
- Accurate quotes require a site visit. A phone quote is not possible on anything beyond a straightforward small lot.

## Voice and Tone Rules

The voice is Jon's voice. Every piece of content should sound like it was written by someone who does this work — not by a marketing department.

**Client-facing documents (proposals, emails, formal follow-up):**
Professional, direct, clear. No filler, no fluff. Plain language a landowner understands without a glossary.

**Social media (Facebook, Instagram):**
Casual, warm, confident. Describes the actual work plainly. Direct call to action, no hype. Real job content only — nothing staged, nothing stock.

**General writing rules — always apply:**
- Sound like a real person, not a template
- Confident without being boastful
- Never oversell — the work speaks for itself
- No emojis. Ever.
- No hashtag overload on social posts

**Banned words and phrases — do not use under any circumstances:**
- "Solutions" (as filler)
- "Industry-leading"
- "Best-in-class"
- "We are passionate about..."
- "Dedicated team"
- "We strive to..."
- "Cutting-edge"
- Any corporate jargon or buzzword
- Any language that inflates a straightforward service

**Voice example (social post):**
> "Took this property from a jungle to something the owner can actually use. Thick cedar, overgrown brush, and a fence line that hadn't seen daylight in years — cleaned it all up with the mulcher and left it looking the way land should look. If your property has gotten away from you, give us a call. We'll get it sorted out."
